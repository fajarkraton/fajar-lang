# Fajar Lang Ambassador Program

> Building a global community of Fajar Lang advocates, educators, and contributors.

---

## What Is an Ambassador?

A Fajar Lang Ambassador is a recognized community leader who helps grow and support the Fajar Lang ecosystem. Ambassadors bridge the gap between the core team and the broader developer community by organizing events, creating educational content, mentoring newcomers, and representing Fajar Lang at conferences and meetups.

Ambassadors are not employees. They are passionate community members who volunteer their time and expertise because they believe in the project's mission: making embedded ML and OS development accessible through a single, safe language.

---

## Requirements

To be considered for the Ambassador program, you must meet **all** of the following:

1. **Active contributor for 3+ months.** This includes code contributions, documentation, bug reports, forum answers, or community support. Consistent engagement matters more than volume.

2. **Demonstrated expertise.** You should be able to explain Fajar Lang's core concepts (context annotations, ownership model, tensor types, pipeline operator) to a technical audience.

3. **Community presence.** You participate in Fajar Lang discussions (GitHub Discussions, Discord, forums) and help other users. A pattern of constructive, respectful engagement is essential.

4. **Content creation or event organization.** You have created at least one of the following:
   - A blog post, tutorial, or video about Fajar Lang
   - A conference talk or meetup presentation
   - An open-source project or library using Fajar Lang
   - A translated documentation page

5. **Adherence to the Code of Conduct.** Ambassadors are held to the highest standard of community behavior. Any Code of Conduct violations disqualify candidates.

---

## Responsibilities

Ambassadors commit to the following on an ongoing basis:

### Community Building
- Organize or co-organize at least one local meetup or online event per quarter.
- Maintain a welcoming presence in community channels.
- Help triage issues labeled `good-first-issue` and `help-wanted` by providing context and mentoring contributors.

### Content and Education
- Produce at least one piece of educational content per quarter (blog post, video, tutorial, talk).
- Keep content technically accurate and up-to-date with the latest Fajar Lang releases.
- Share Fajar Lang updates on social media and professional networks.

### Feedback and Advocacy
- Provide feedback on pre-release features, RFCs, and language design decisions.
- Report community sentiment and pain points to the core team.
- Represent Fajar Lang honestly — highlight strengths without hiding limitations.

### Mentoring
- Mentor at least one new contributor through their first pull request.
- Participate in code review for community-submitted PRs when available.

---

## Benefits

### Early Access
- Preview builds of new Fajar Lang releases (beta channel).
- Early access to RFC drafts and design documents.
- Direct communication channel with the core team.

### Recognition
- Listed on the official Fajar Lang website as an Ambassador.
- Ambassador badge on GitHub Discussions and community platforms.
- Annual "Ambassador Spotlight" blog post featuring your contributions.

### Swag and Support
- Official Fajar Lang merchandise (t-shirts, stickers, pins) shipped annually.
- Fajar Lang branded slide templates for presentations.
- Priority support for technical questions related to talks or content.

### Conference Sponsorship
- Up to $500 USD travel stipend per year for speaking about Fajar Lang at approved conferences (subject to budget availability).
- Conference ticket reimbursement when presenting a Fajar Lang talk.
- Co-branding support for conference booths or demo stations.

## Application Process

### Step 1: Self-Assessment

Review the requirements above. If you meet all five criteria, proceed to Step 2.

### Step 2: Submit Application

Open a GitHub Discussion in the `Community` category with the title: **"Ambassador Application: [Your Name]"**

Include the following:

```markdown
## Ambassador Application

**Name:** [Your full name or handle]
**Location:** [City, Country]
**GitHub:** [Your GitHub username]
**Active since:** [Month/Year you started contributing]

### Contributions
[List your contributions: PRs, issues, blog posts, talks, translations, etc.]

### Why I Want to Be an Ambassador
[2-3 paragraphs about your motivation and what you plan to do]

### Planned Activities
[Specific activities you plan to undertake in the first 6 months]
```

### Step 3: Review

The core team reviews applications monthly. We evaluate:
- Quality and consistency of past contributions
- Proposed activity plan
- Geographic and expertise diversity (we aim for global coverage)
- Community references (other contributors who can vouch for you)

### Step 4: Onboarding

Accepted ambassadors receive:
- A welcome email with program details and resources.
- Access to the Ambassador private channel.
- An onboarding call with a core team member.
- The official Ambassador badge on community platforms.

---

## Term and Renewal

- Ambassador status is granted for a **12-month term**.
- At the end of each term, ambassadors may apply for renewal by summarizing their activities.
- Ambassadors who are inactive for 6+ months may be moved to "Alumni" status.
- Alumni are always welcome to re-apply.
